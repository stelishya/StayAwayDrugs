Say No to Drugs

Your mind is your power. Don’t let drugs steal it.

In a world driven by innovation, creativity, and technology, our greatest asset is a clear and focused mind. Yet, many promising talents in the tech world fall prey to a silent enemy — DRUGS.

Impacts

1. Students

Why they start : Peer pressure, curiosity, academic stress.
Effects:
  • Drop in academic performance.
  • Reduced concentration and memory.
  • Increased absenteeism and risk of dropping out.
  • Long-term brain development issues.

2. Working Professionals / Techies

Why they start : Stress, burnout, isolation, performance pressure.
Effects:
  • Decreased productivity and focus.
  • Missed deadlines and job loss.
  • Poor decision-making in critical tasks.
  • Health decline leads to absenteeism and financial instability.

3. Parents

Why they start : Depression, life stress, emotional trauma.
Effects:
  • Neglect of children and household responsibilities.
  • Financial issues from spending on substances.
  • Emotional and sometimes physical abuse in families.
  • Risk of passing addiction behavior to children.

4. Elderly

Why they start : Chronic pain, loneliness, overuse of prescriptions.
Effects:
  • Increased risk of falls and injuries.
  • Worsening of mental health (confusion, memory loss).
  • Dangerous drug interactions due to polypharmacy.

5. Teenagers / Youth

Why they start : Curiosity, rebellion, social media influence.
Effects:
  • High risk of developing lifelong addiction.
  • Behavior issues and conflicts with parents/schools.
  • Involvement in risky or criminal behavior.
  • Impaired brain development during critical years.

6. Pregnant Women

Why they start : Effects on mother and baby:
Effects:
  • Miscarriage or premature labor.
  • Life-threatening complications.
  • Birth defects.
  • Low birth weight.

Overview

Drugs Destroys Techies is a bold initiative aimed at raising awareness about the devastating impact of substance abuse among developers, engineers, students, and tech enthusiasts. Whether it's the pressure of deadlines, burnout, loneliness, or the false belief that substances enhance productivity—drug abuse only leads to broken dreams, damaged health, and derailed careers. This platform serves as a wake-up call to the tech community: Your mind is your power. Don’t let drugs steal it. Through real stories, expert advice, resources, and community support, Drugs Destroys Techies encourages individuals to choose clarity over chaos, and code with consciousness—not chemicals.

April 29 — National Fentanyl Awareness Day

Do you know where your pill or powder came from? Unless you got it from a pharmacy, it could contain a lethal dose of fentanyl. Protect yourself and your friends by learning the facts and spreading the word on April 29, National Fentanyl Awareness Day.

National Prescription Drug Take Back Day is a safe, convenient, and responsible way to dispose of unused or expired prescription drugs. The next Take Back Day is April 26, 2025 from 10am-2pm (local time) at locations nationwide.
